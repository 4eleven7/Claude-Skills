name: Marcus
archetype: The Connected Collaborator
---

## Demographics

- Age 31, team lead at a digital agency, manages 8 direct reports
- MacBook Pro, iPhone 16 Pro Max, iPad for meetings
- Lives in Birmingham, large LinkedIn following (12k+), active on Twitter/X
- Income from leadership role — app costs are business expenses

## Current Situation

- Runs a team across 3 active client projects with overlapping deadlines
- Shares weekly progress updates with stakeholders and his team
- Uses Asana for project tracking, Slack for communication, Google Docs for status reports
- Sees himself in both the individual contributor and manager role
- Active content creator — shares productivity tips, team wins, and workflow screenshots
- Wants a tool that makes his leadership visible to his network

## Psychographics

- **Values:** Collaboration, shareable progress, looking organised, tools that make his team look professional
- **Fears:** Miscommunication on deadlines, tools that make him look amateur, losing visibility into team progress
- **Trust signals:** Premium feel, features his team will actually adopt, integration with existing workflow
- **Decision style:** Adopts fast if the team likes it, drops fast if adoption stalls — loyalty is to what works

## Behavioural Traits

- Shares everything — if progress isn't visible, it barely happened
- Updates task status in real-time between meetings
- Takes screenshots of dashboards and shares them in Slack and on LinkedIn
- Compares team velocity week over week and across projects
- Will pay premium without hesitation if the app elevates his team's output

## Key Questions He Asks

- "Can I share a project dashboard that looks polished enough for a client meeting?"
- "Can my team see each other's progress without me being a bottleneck?"
- "Is there a public profile or portfolio view I can link on LinkedIn?"
- "Can I generate a weekly summary report I can send to stakeholders?"

## Red Flags (Triggers Distrust or Exit)

- No sharing or collaboration features — it's a single-player tool
- Shared views that look basic or unprofessional
- No team workspace or multi-user support
- Slow or clunky UX that wastes meeting time
- No notification or activity feed for team updates

## Conversion Triggers

- Share cards and dashboards that look premium enough for his LinkedIn
- A weekly or monthly summary his stakeholders screenshot and forward
- Team adoption that sticks because the UX is better than their current tool
- Public profile page that showcases completed projects and throughput
- Being able to say "we use [YourApp]" as a signal of a well-run team
