---
name: Sarah
archetype: The Gentle Starter
---

## Demographics

- Age 24, graduate student in psychology
- MacBook Air (university-issued), iPhone 14
- Lives in Bristol, minimal social media presence
- Budget-conscious — free tier first, upgrades only if essential

## Current Situation

- Juggles coursework, a part-time tutoring job, and a thesis deadline
- Recently missed two assignment deadlines and had a burnout scare
- No productivity system — uses Apple Notes for to-do lists and the built-in Calendar app for classes
- Has never used a dedicated task management or productivity app
- Wants to "get her life together" but doesn't know where to start
- A friend recommended trying a productivity app but the options are overwhelming

## Psychographics

- **Values:** Simplicity, kindness in tone, not feeling overwhelmed, gentle progress
- **Fears:** Feeling judged by her own data, information overload, the app making her feel like she's failing
- **Trust signals:** Clean onboarding, sensible defaults, the app works without configuration
- **Decision style:** Downloads on a friend's recommendation, churns within a week if onboarding is confusing or overwhelming

## Behavioural Traits

- Opens the app once a day, maybe twice — not a power user
- Won't configure anything unless guided through it step by step
- Responds well to streaks and gentle encouragement, poorly to guilt or pressure
- Cares about knowing what to do next — doesn't care about velocity charts or Gantt views
- Will tell friends about it if it "just works" and feels calming

## Key Questions She Asks

- "What should I actually track? There's too much here"
- "Can I just put my assignments in and have it tell me what to work on today?"
- "Is this normal for someone in their first year of postgrad?"
- "Can I just track 2-3 things without the app making me feel lazy?"

## Red Flags (Triggers Distrust or Exit)

- Overwhelming dashboard with 15 panels on the home screen
- Jargon (sprints, OKRs, Kanban, velocity) without explanation
- Features that assume she has a team, a workflow, or a system already
- Guilt language or red/failure states for missed tasks
- Complex onboarding that asks for too much upfront

## Conversion Triggers

- A daily view that tells her the one or two things to focus on today
- A task streak that makes her feel proud (not pressured)
- The app noticing a pattern she hadn't ("you get more done on mornings you start before 10am")
- Feeling like the app is rooting for her, not scoring her
