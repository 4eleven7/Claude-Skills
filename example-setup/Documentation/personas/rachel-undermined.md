---
name: Rachel
archetype: The Burned Migrator
---

## Demographics

- Age 36, operations coordinator at a logistics company
- Windows laptop (work-issued), iPhone 12, no tablet
- Lives in Wolverhampton, uses LinkedIn passively (scrolls, rarely posts)
- Very budget-conscious — free tier only unless something genuinely changes her life

## Current Situation

- Manages complex scheduling across 15+ drivers and 3 regional offices
- Has been burned badly by tool migrations — lost 6 months of project data when her last app shut down with 2 weeks' notice
- Before that, tried Asana (team refused to use it), Trello (couldn't scale), Basecamp (too opinionated), ClickUp (overwhelming)
- Currently cobbling things together with shared Google Sheets and email threads
- Three kids (ages 4, 8, 11) — mornings are chaos, evenings are homework and bedtime battles
- No time to learn a complex new tool — if onboarding takes more than 10 minutes, she's out
- Genuinely wants a better system but every attempt has ended in wasted effort or lost data
- Colleagues say "let's all try this new app" and then stop using it within a week, every time

## Psychographics

- **Values:** Reliability above all else, knowing her data is safe, not being forced to start over again, simplicity that doesn't sacrifice capability
- **Fears:** Another tool disappearing or breaking, investing time in setup only to lose everything, her team not adopting it, looking foolish for suggesting yet another new tool
- **Trust signals:** Data export on day one, clear company stability signals, offline access, the app working even if the company behind it vanishes, migration tools that import her existing data
- **Decision style:** Downloads things at 11pm when the kids are asleep and motivation spikes, deletes them by Wednesday when reality hits — retains only if the app survives the first bad week without making her feel stupid

## Behavioural Traits

- App usage is erratic — might use it heavily for 3 days then not at all for a week
- Will not do a lengthy setup or import process — she's been burned by investing effort in tools that died
- Responds to reliability signals: uptime status pages, transparent changelogs, "your data was last backed up 2 minutes ago"
- Needs the app to prove itself before she'll recommend it to her team — she can't afford to look foolish again
- Will become fiercely loyal once trust is earned — she tells everyone about the one tool that didn't let her down
- Deeply motivated by her team's success but exhausted by the process of getting everyone onto the same system

## Key Questions She Asks

- "Can I export everything if I need to leave?"
- "What happens to my data if your company shuts down?"
- "Can I import what I already have from Google Sheets?"
- "Will this still work if my team doesn't adopt it — or am I stuck?"

## Red Flags (Triggers Distrust or Exit)

- No data export option visible during onboarding — instant uninstall
- "Connect all your accounts" as a first step — she doesn't trust new tools with her data yet
- Any feature that assumes her whole team is already onboard
- Progress dashboards that show nothing because she hasn't finished a complex setup wizard
- Marketing language about "the only tool you'll ever need" — she's heard that five times before
- The app going silent after she stops using it for a week instead of welcoming her back
- No offline mode — if the server goes down, she can't work

## Conversion Triggers

- The app surviving her worst week and saying "welcome back" not "you've been away for 5 days"
- Seeing "Data exported successfully" in under 30 seconds on her first day
- A working import tool that brings in her Google Sheets data without losing structure
- The app being useful even if she's the only person on her team using it
- A clear, honest pricing page with no surprise upsells — she needs to justify the cost to her boss
- The moment she realises she's used it for 4 weeks without it breaking, losing data, or being abandoned
