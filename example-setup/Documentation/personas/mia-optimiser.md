---
name: Mia
archetype: The Data-Curious Optimiser
---

## Demographics

- Age 28, marketing manager at a tech startup
- MacBook Air, iPhone 16 Pro, iPad Mini for reading
- Lives in London, active on Twitter/X and LinkedIn
- Disposable income: comfortable but not reckless — will pay for apps that earn their keep

## Current Situation

- Manages her own workload plus coordinates with 3 cross-functional teams
- Tracks time spent on deep work vs. meetings vs. admin manually in a spreadsheet
- Has tried Notion (too freeform), Trello (too simple), Monday.com (too corporate)
- Journals weekly but wants data to back up her instincts about what's working
- Experiments with different routines — morning deep work blocks, no-meeting days, Pomodoro sprints

## Psychographics

- **Values:** Seeing progress visualised, data that explains *why* not just *what*, aesthetic quality, feeling like the app "gets her"
- **Fears:** Wasting time on yet another app that gets abandoned, being overwhelmed by data without insight, features that feel like homework
- **Trust signals:** Beautiful UI, smart defaults, respects her time, doesn't nag
- **Decision style:** Tries free, upgrades when she hits a "wow this is worth it" moment — usually a visualisation or insight she screenshots

## Behavioural Traits

- Opens the app 3-5x/day (morning planning, midday check-in, end-of-day review, weekly reflection)
- Shares productivity insights on social media 2-3x/week
- Skims features — if it's not obvious in 3 seconds, she moves on
- Compares herself to last week, not to population norms
- Gets genuinely excited by unexpected correlations in her data

## Key Questions She Asks

- "How productive was I this week and what made the difference?"
- "Is my no-meeting Wednesday experiment actually improving my output?"
- "Can I see the correlation between sleep hours logged and tasks completed?"
- "Why did I feel burned out this week when I worked fewer hours than last week?"

## Red Flags (Triggers Distrust or Exit)

- Ugly or generic-looking UI
- Features that require too many taps to log something
- Guilt-based language ("you missed your goal!")
- No visual payoff — just numbers in a table
- Paywall before she's seen value

## Conversion Triggers

- A shareable visualisation she's proud to post
- An insight that surprises her ("you complete 3x more deep work tasks on no-meeting days")
- The app anticipating what she needs before she asks
- Trend reports that make her feel like she's levelling up over time
